About me

I am a PhD student in Department of Computer Science at Rutgers University. Before coming to Rutgers, I obtained my Bachelor degree from Northeastern University in China. My research interests broadly lie in mobile, sensing and networked systems in the scope of smart cities, with a major focus on vehicular mobility modeling. My advisor is Professor Desheng Zhang.

Projects

Vehicular Mobility Modeling on A Large Scale

[MobiCom'19][UbiComp'18]

By combining static sensors (ETC system) and mobile sensors (probe vehicles), we aim to model the state level vehicular mobility.

Your Search Tells Others Where to Park

[UbiComp'18][Dataset]

We estimate the parking availability based on the human parking behavior model. The novelty is we can estimate the background parking availability (the parking slot not parked by participated vehicles) when a vehicle passing the slot.

Inferring Air Quality in Urban Sensing Systems

[Sensors]

We design a random forest based algorithm to infer the air quality using data from air quality mornitor stations, meteorology data, traffic report and poi distribution.
